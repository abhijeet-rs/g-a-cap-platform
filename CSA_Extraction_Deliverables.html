The CSA Problem
The Customer Service Agreement (CSA) is the foundational document for every client onboarding. It defines the entire contractual relationship -- fee structures, services, SUTA coverage, billing exceptions, WC codes, tax classifications, and term details. Today, 4 independent manual reviews of the same CSA PDF are performed by separate team members (PM Manager, PM, PA Manager, PA), each spending 30-40 minutes manually sifting through a multi-page legal document. No structured output is shared between reviewers.
The Integration Gap
There is no Salesforce-to-ClientSpace integration. Business Analysts manually re-enter deal data from Salesforce into ClientSpace, frequently entering placeholder data in required fields to satisfy system requirements. These placeholders create discrepancies that are only discovered during downstream onboarding meetings, causing delays, rework, and repeated manual cross-referencing across systems.
Key Insight: The CSA review process alone accounts for approximately 2% of PM time and 1% of PA time across the entire team. When combined with Sales Handoff (which depends on validated CSA data), the total impact reaches 7% PM and 3% PA time allocation -- representing 1.6 FTEs of capacity that could be reclaimed through automation.
Operational Context
Volume: G&A Partners onboards approximately 704 clients per year with a team of 35 FTEs (15 Project Managers, 16 Payroll Analysts, 4 Leadership/Management). Total onboarding FLC is $4.5M.

Cycle Time: Current onboarding takes 40-60 days from CSA signing to first payroll -- nearly double the industry standard of approximately 3 weeks. Compressing this cycle by 10-14 calendar days would directly accelerate gross margin recognition.

The CSA's Role: Every downstream activity -- system configuration in PrismHR, billing rule setup, WC code assignment, SUTA rate configuration, document collection -- depends on accurate extraction and validation of CSA data. Errors introduced at this stage cascade through the entire onboarding lifecycle.

Revenue Impact: Each day of onboarding delay means one fewer day of billable payroll cycles for G&A. With 704 clients per year and an average cycle compression target of 10-14 days, the gross margin capture opportunity is significant.

G. Business Flow Understanding
Current Business Process -- Detailed Explanation
When a sale closes at G&A Partners, the Business Analyst creates a Client Service Agreement (CSA) PDF that documents every aspect of the client relationship. This document flows through the onboarding division where it undergoes four sequential manual reviews:

1. PM Manager CSA Review (30-40 min): The PM Manager manually reads the entire CSA to understand fee structure, services included/excluded, billing exceptions, effective dates, and term. They cross-reference this against the ClientSpace handoff page fields entered by the BA.

2. PM Sales Handoff Review: During the structured sales handoff meeting, the PM conducts their own independent review of the same CSA, validating scope, services, and client expectations against what was entered in ClientSpace.

3. PA Manager CSA Review: The PA Manager performs a third independent review focused on payroll-specific terms -- SUTA coverage type, WC codes, pay frequency, tax classifications, and billing rules.

4. PA CSA Review: The assigned Payroll Analyst performs the fourth review, again extracting the same payroll-relevant fields to configure PrismHR.

How AI-Assisted Extraction Solves Each Problem
What Gets Automated
PDF Field Extraction: AI reads the CSA PDF and extracts 20+ structured fields -- company name, FEIN, effective date, admin fee, services, SUTA coverage, WC codes, term, billing exceptions, tax classification, ownership structure, cyber policy status.

Cross-Validation: Extracted fields are automatically compared against Salesforce deal data and ClientSpace handoff page fields. Every mismatch is flagged with source attribution (which system has which value) and confidence scores.

Discrepancy Flagging: Placeholder values, missing required fields, and data inconsistencies are surfaced immediately at deal close -- not days later during onboarding meetings.

Initial Data Population: Validated, high-confidence fields are pre-populated into the ClientSpace onboarding record, eliminating manual data entry for the majority of fields.
Where Humans Remain in the Loop
Approval Gate: No data flows to ClientSpace without explicit human approval. A reviewer must sign off on the extraction results before any system updates occur.

Edge Cases: Non-standard CSA formats, handwritten amendments, complex multi-entity agreements, and unusual clause structures are routed to senior reviewers with partial extraction results as a starting point.

Low-Confidence Fields: Fields extracted with confidence below 0.80 are flagged for manual verification rather than auto-populated. Fields below 0.60 are flagged as potential hallucinations.

Mismatch Resolution: When the cross-validation engine flags a genuine discrepancy between CSA, Salesforce, and ClientSpace, a human must determine which source is authoritative and resolve the conflict.
Expected Business Impact: 85% reduction in CSA review time across the onboarding team. Four independent 30-40 minute reviews replaced by a single 5-10 minute validation step reviewing pre-populated, AI-extracted data with flagged mismatches. Faster onboarding cycle through immediate discrepancy resolution at deal close rather than discovery during downstream meetings.

Solution Architecture
AI-Assisted CSA Extraction & Cross-Validation
A seven-layer architecture designed for enterprise-grade document extraction, multi-system cross-validation, human-in-the-loop approval, and secure integration with ClientSpace. Each layer is independently scalable and testable.

Layer Details
Layer 1
Ingestion Layer
Purpose: Accept CSA PDF uploads, validate file integrity, detect document type, and queue for processing.

Components: File upload endpoint (multipart/form-data), file type validation (PDF, scanned PDF detection), file size limits (50MB max), virus scanning, duplicate detection (SHA-256 hash), document type classification (CSA vs. amendment vs. addendum), processing queue (async job creation).

Technology: FastAPI upload endpoint, Redis/Celery task queue, S3-compatible object storage for document persistence. File metadata stored in PostgreSQL with upload timestamp, uploader identity, and processing status.
Layer 2
AI Extraction Pipeline
Purpose: Convert unstructured CSA PDF content into structured, typed fields with per-field confidence scores.

Pipeline Stages:
1. PDF Parsing (LlamaParse): LlamaParse converts CSA PDFs into per-page structured Markdown with OCR items including bounding box coordinates. Handles native and scanned PDFs with high fidelity. API-key-based integration for fast setup.
2. Intelligent Field Extraction (Claude Haiku): Claude Haiku via the Anthropic API performs LLM-based intelligent extraction of 42+ fields from the parsed Markdown. Each field is returned with its page number, source citation, and confidence score. Domain-specific extraction prompts tuned for PEO/HRO terminology.
3. Word-Level OCR (Tesseract): Tesseract OCR processes each PDF page to produce word-level bounding boxes (7000+ words per page), enabling precise in-document highlighting of extracted field locations.
4. 3-Tier Bounding Box Cascade: A three-pass matching algorithm resolves extracted field text to precise document positions: LINE pass (exact line match from LlamaParse OCR items), WORD-run pass (consecutive word sequence matching from Tesseract output), and Loose-line pass (relaxed matching for partial or reformatted text).
5. Confidence Scoring: Each extracted field receives a confidence score (0.0-1.0) based on extraction clarity, format compliance, and cross-field consistency.

Technology: Primary: LlamaParse (PDF → per-page Markdown with OCR items) + Claude Haiku via Anthropic API (intelligent field extraction) + Tesseract OCR (word-level bounding box resolution). Production: Azure AI Document Intelligence for OCR + Claude for semantic extraction.
Layer 3
Cross-Validation Engine
Purpose: Compare extracted CSA fields against Salesforce deal data and ClientSpace handoff page fields to detect mismatches, placeholder values, and missing required data.

Validation Types:
- Exact Match: FEIN, effective date, term length -- must match exactly across systems
- Semantic Match: Fee descriptions, service names -- normalize before comparison ("$45 per employee per month" = "$45/ee/mo")
- Range Validation: Admin fees within expected ranges for service tier, SUTA rates within state-specific bounds
- Completeness Check: All required fields populated with non-placeholder values
- Consistency Check: Cross-field logic (e.g., if SUTA coverage = "client-responsible", SUTA rates should not be populated in billing)

Output: Validation report with per-field status (match/mismatch/missing), source values from each system, confidence assessment, and recommended resolution.
Layer 4
Human Review & Approval Workflow
Purpose: Document-centric review experience for validation, correction, and approval of extracted CSA data.

CSA Dashboard: Every uploaded CSA appears as a row with real-time status tracking following a clear upload → processing spinner → eye icon flow. Processing CSAs show a spinner; completed extractions enable an Eye icon to open the review drawer. Auto-refreshes every 5 seconds.

Side Drawer: Full-height right-side drawer with two-panel split — left panel (45%) shows editable extracted fields grouped by section with confidence scores, validation badges, notes, and change tracking. Right panel (55%) shows the CSA PDF rendered via PDF.js with viewer controls (zoom, rotate, search, page navigation). In-document yellow highlights mark extracted field locations using percentage-positioned div overlays with mixBlendMode: multiply. Deep-linkable via URL parameters.

Approval Modal: Approval triggers a cross-validation summary modal showing Salesforce mismatches and ClientSpace sync actions before final confirmation. This Salesforce→ClientSpace bridge means approval replaces manual BA re-entry. Approved CSAs are locked — re-editing requires an explicit unlock with a downstream impact warning ("Changes will modify ClientSpace entries"). Field-level audit trail tracks every edit by persona.

Approval Gates: No data flows to ClientSpace without explicit approval. Low-confidence fields require individual sign-off. Mismatch resolutions require the reviewer to select the authoritative source.
Layer 5
ClientSpace Integration Adapter
Purpose: Translate approved extraction results into ClientSpace API calls, managing the full sync lifecycle from payload construction through confirmation.

Components: API client with OAuth token management, DTO mapper (extraction schema to ClientSpace field schema), payload builder, response normalizer, retry handler (exponential backoff with jitter, max 3 retries), error classifier (transient vs. permanent vs. validation), idempotency key generator, and audit logger.

Technology: Python httpx async client, Pydantic models for DTO validation, circuit breaker pattern for API resilience. Detailed in Tab 6.
Layer 6
Security & Masking Layer
Purpose: Protect PII and sensitive client data throughout the extraction and review pipeline.

Capabilities: Customer name aliasing (real names replaced with codenames in demo/review UIs), SSN masking (XXX-XX-1234), FEIN partial masking, bank account full masking, address reduction (city/state only), role-based access control for unmasked data access. All masking is applied at the presentation layer; raw data is stored encrypted at rest with field-level encryption for PII fields.

Technology: AES-256 field-level encryption, AWS KMS or Azure Key Vault for key management, middleware-based masking in the API layer. Detailed in Tab 5.
Layer 7
Audit & Monitoring Layer
Purpose: Maintain an immutable audit trail of all system activities and monitor extraction quality over time.

Audit Events: Every extraction logged (who uploaded, when, what was extracted, confidence scores). Every human override logged (original value, new value, reason, reviewer identity). Every approval/rejection logged. Every ClientSpace sync attempt logged with request/response. All events are append-only (immutable log).

Monitoring: Real-time dashboards for extraction volume, average confidence, human correction rate, sync success rate, and processing latency. Alerting for confidence drift (model degradation), sync failure spikes, and queue depth anomalies.

Section J - Evaluation Strategy
Comprehensive Evaluation Strategy
Extraction Quality & Cross-Validation Accuracy
A multi-dimensional evaluation framework covering field-level accuracy, table extraction quality, entity recognition, cross-validation precision, and a golden dataset strategy for continuous model improvement. Every metric has a defined target and measurement methodology.
Field-Level Accuracy Evals
Exact Match Rate
Measures whether the extracted value is character-for-character identical to the ground truth annotation. Applied to structured fields: FEIN, effective dates, admin fee amounts, term length. This is the strictest accuracy measure and the primary metric for fields that must be precisely correct (e.g., FEIN must be exactly 9 digits, no partial credit).
Semantic Match Rate
Measures whether the extracted value conveys the same meaning as the ground truth after normalization. Examples: "$45 per employee per month" matches "$45/ee/mo". "Workers' Compensation" matches "WC". "Thirty-six months" matches "36 months". Applied to descriptive fields where format variation is expected but meaning must be preserved.
Confidence Calibration
Measures the correlation between predicted confidence and actual accuracy. A well-calibrated model should be correct 95% of the time on fields where it predicts 0.95 confidence. Evaluated using reliability diagrams (calibration curves) plotting predicted probability vs. observed accuracy across confidence bins. Target: Pearson correlation > 0.85 between predicted confidence and actual accuracy.
Per-Field Error Analysis
Breakdown of extraction errors by field type to identify systematic weaknesses. Tracks error rates for each of the 20+ CSA fields independently, enabling targeted improvement. Fields with consistently low accuracy are flagged for additional training data, prompt engineering, or fallback to manual entry. Error distribution reveals whether failures are random or field-specific.

Percentage of flagged mismatches that are genuine discrepancies requiring resolution. Target: >88%. Higher is better -- indicates the system is not wasting reviewer time on false alarms.

Percentage of flagged mismatches that are actually correct (format differences, acceptable synonyms, etc.). Target: <12%. Lower is better -- excessive false positives erode reviewer trust and increase review time.

Percentage of real mismatches that the system failed to flag. Target: <5%. This is the most critical metric -- missed mismatches flow to ClientSpace uncorrected. Current manual baseline is estimated at ~30%.

Golden Dataset Strategy
Dataset Construction
Size: Collect 50 representative CSAs across different complexity levels. This provides sufficient statistical power for per-field accuracy measurement while being feasible for expert annotation.

Ground Truth Creation: Two expert BAs independently annotate each CSA with structured field values. Disagreements are resolved by a senior reviewer (adjudication). Final annotations become the ground truth labels.

Stratification: The dataset is stratified into three complexity tiers:
- Simple (40%): Standard services, single state, standard fee schedule (20 CSAs)
- Moderate (35%): Custom fees, multi-state operations, some billing exceptions (17-18 CSAs)
- Complex (25%): Multi-entity, extensive exceptions, custom clauses, multi-state SUTA variations (12-13 CSAs)

Versioning: The dataset is version-controlled with each annotation revision tracked. Model performance is evaluated against each dataset version to measure improvement over time.

Upload Guardrails
PDF Validation at Upload
Before any file reaches the server, the upload page performs client-side validation:
• PDF header check — reads the first bytes to verify the %PDF magic header
• File size limit — rejects files over 25 MB

• Encrypted PDF detection — scans for /Encrypt marker and rejects password-protected files
• File type enforcement — only accepts .pdf files, rejects all other formats

Invalid files are rejected with a clear error banner — no upload is initiated.

Adapter Component Design
API Client
HTTP client with automatic OAuth token refresh, connection pooling, request/response logging, configurable timeouts (30s default), and circuit breaker pattern (open after 5 consecutive failures, half-open retry after 60s).
DTO Mapper
Maps the internal extraction schema to ClientSpace field names and types. Handles type coercion (string to date, string to decimal), enum mapping (service codes to CS-specific codes), and null handling (required vs. optional field rules).
Payload Builder
Constructs valid ClientSpace API request payloads from mapped DTOs. Handles nested object construction, array serialization, multipart form data for document uploads, and request signing where required.
Response Normalizer
Standardizes ClientSpace API responses into a consistent internal format regardless of endpoint-specific response structures. Extracts created entity IDs, error messages, validation failures, and rate limit headers.
Retry Handler & Error Classifier
Retry: Exponential backoff with jitter (base: 1s, max: 30s, max retries: 3). Error Classification: Transient errors (5xx, timeout, network) trigger retry. Permanent errors (4xx auth, 404) are logged and routed to human. Validation errors (400 with field details) trigger fix-and-retry.
Idempotency & Audit
Idempotency Key: SHA256(document_id + field_set + timestamp_bucket) ensures duplicate API calls are safe. Audit Logger: Every API call attempt, response, and outcome is logged with correlation ID linking back to the source extraction and approval event.

Salesforce-ClientSpace Bridge
Bridging the Integration Gap
Since there is no direct Salesforce-to-ClientSpace integration, the CSA extraction system serves as the de facto bridge. The data flow is:

Salesforce (read-only) -- Pull deal data via Salesforce REST API (opportunity fields, account details, BA-entered values)
CSA Extracted Fields -- AI extracts the contractual source of truth from the PDF
Cross-Validation Engine -- Compares SF values vs. CSA values vs. existing CS values
Normalized Profile -- Creates a single validated record with provenance tracking (which system each value came from)
ClientSpace Sync -- Approved profile synced to CS via integration adapter

This effectively creates a unidirectional SF-to-CS pipeline mediated by the CSA as the authoritative document, with AI extraction eliminating the manual re-entry that currently introduces errors.
Approval Modal as the Bridge: The Approval Modal presents a cross-validation summary showing Salesforce mismatches alongside ClientSpace sync actions before the reviewer confirms. This single approval step replaces the manual BA re-entry workflow, serving as the Salesforce→ClientSpace bridge with full field-level provenance tracking.
