## ClientSpace discussion

- **ClientSpace was positioned as the system of record after client approval.** Once the benefits booklet is signed, the updated information should sync back into ClientSpace so all client documentation and contract details live there.

- **It was described as one of the final major steps in the workflow.** The reviewer simplified the story into a small number of top-level steps and said that after generating the booklet and getting client approval, the update should be pushed back to ClientSpace. They compared it to “push the update back to Salesforce,” but for this flow the destination was ClientSpace.

- **ClientSpace also came up as the expected source for some client-specific input data.** One point of feedback was that the demo should not imply users are manually uploading census data if that information should already exist in ClientSpace. The expectation expressed was that “the census everything should be pulled from the client space.”

- **That means ClientSpace played two roles in the conversation:**
  - as an **input/source system** for client data like census/CSA-related information, and
  - as an **output/system of record** where approved CAP information and related documentation should be synced back after sign-off.

- **In the proposed four-module demo structure, ClientSpace belonged mainly in Module 4.** That module was framed as the “closing the loop” step: after signature, sync to ClientSpace and Prism. ClientSpace would hold the documentation/contract record, while Prism would handle updated rates, plans, and enrollments.

- **There was also a narrative simplification angle.** The advice was to keep the story very clear for the CEO: generate CAP, generate booklet, get signature, then sync back into ClientSpace. The point was not to overcomplicate this with too much system detail too early in the demo.
